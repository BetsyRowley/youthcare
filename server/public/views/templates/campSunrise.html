Each Summer 250-300 youth participate in Camp Sunrise's summer and school year activities. In addition to week-long summer sessions, Camp Sunrise hosts leadership retreats and urban outdoor recreation activities during the school year.

Demographic Information

Camp Sunrise works with about 250 youth ages 13-18 from Minneapolis and St. Paul in the following general demographic breakdown:

  • African/African American - 49%
  • Asian American - 32%
  • Native American - 2%
  • European American - 4%
  • Latino/a - 3%
  • Multiracial - 10%

Awards and Recognition

In addition to numerouse media feature, Camp Sunrise has recieved significant recognition for its impact on the community over the years.

2014 Certificate of Recognition from Minnesota Governor Mark Dayton for participation in the Adopt-a-River program on the St. Croix River.

2008 Access to the Outdoors Award from the Parks & Trails Council of Minnesota

2000 Nonprofit Mission Award for Anit-Racism from the Minnesota Council of Nonprofits

1993 National Park Service Certificate of Appreciation for outstanding contributions to the St. Croix National Scenic Riverway

1991 American Red Cross Community Service Award

1990 Minnesota Clean River Project Certificate of Commendation from Minnesota Governor Rudy Perpich

1989 President's Environmental Youth Award from President George H. Bush

1988 VIP Volunteers in Parks Program from St. Croix Wild and Scenic River

1987 President's Citation for Private Sector Initiatives from President Ronald Reagan

1986 Distinguished Service Award from the Mayor of Minneapolis

1985 Eleanor P. Eells Award for Program Excellence from the Fund for the Advancement of Camping
